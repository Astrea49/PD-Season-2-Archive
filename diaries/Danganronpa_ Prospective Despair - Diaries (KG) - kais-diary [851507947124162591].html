June 16th, XXXX Entry 3 To think that so much has happened in six days is . . . interesting? To say the least. So many thoughts I have . . . so many events I experienced . . . so many people to be weary about. If I can recall, someone became a victim to death a few days ago. The handbook said his name was Benjamin. I've never met him once, however, seeing his corpse lay still in a pool of his own dried blood was enough to make me feel immense dread. Everyone else must've felt the same way - some more than others. People I thought that would avoid the room Benjamin was in instead waltzed inside as if they'd seen him there before. I can't say that none of them were unfazed by the sight of the deceased, but they acted accordingly - becoming little detectives of their own and actually looking for clues to get justice for him. I wish I could've done the same. But . . . I sought comfort from my deity. At . . . least I wasn't freaking out as much as I thought I would. We were forced to go to trial to discuss what we've learnt about Benjamin's murder. I didn't get to actually sit down and read over what was on the handbook about the case like I said I would. I . . . really did not want to involve myself in that anymore. Besides, the most I could've done then was memorize the clues word for word - I wouldn't have motivated myself to analyze anything. As a result of my negligence . . . I was useless. Everyone managed to catch the killer anyway . . . without me it seems. . . . (edited)
The killer was Kento by the way. He was executed horribly. Just seeing the grotsque nature of how Kento's body was mauled . . . I don't want to die like that. Immediately after, the aircraft landed at an abandoned military base. Everyone was eager to explore, understandably enough. Having to stay in the same place where two people have been brutally put to death was enough to send anyone crazy. Noriko felt the same way . . . I think . . . so we both left together as soon as possible. We bonded a little. I hope we get to speak more. . . . (edited)
Yesterday morning, I was awoken by a sort of whirring noise. I initially thought the aircraft was preparing to rise back into the sky . . . but the door to the cargo bay was still open. I wanted to investigate . . . and so did many others apparently. By the time I came outside, I couldn't believe what I saw. There was a fighter jet in the air . . . and it was here to rescue us! Or so I thought. Drake effortlessly shot down each and every single jet that came for us. I wanted to cry. Even worse was that I had a bad run in with Jack again. He couldn't comply with what I asked of him awhile ago, and looked like he wanted me dead. He's upset that I had to use my lighter to save myself from another assault . . . he's just horrible. A selfish, egotistical prick that only thinks of himself. I was a fool for thinking otherwise. I was a fool. I was a fool. I was a fool. . . . the world needs no more fools like he and I. Signed, At'ééd Anádlohí (edited)

The second motive. A way to show my true self. One will be out lived. Boffin, Wood Carver. Sacrificed with no remorse. Several left to play. A nighttime visit. Nighttime interrogation. Nighttime butterflies. Boffin's paradise. Checked with pain and suffering. Checked with TLC. A candymaker. A towering lumberjack. Both were just pleasant. Blake Blake Blake Blake Blake. Blake Blake Blake Blake Blake Blake Blake. Sorry. I'm tired. I don't remember anything else directly after these memories though. Therefore, I will skip to what I know of recently. . . .
The next deaths were Takuya, Kojima, Ema, Luna, and Tokumei. In that order. I wouldn't say I was particularly close with any one of them, but I wouldn't say I was too far either. What I can say for certain was that I was once again no help with any of their deaths. Really, what the fuck am I doing? After the army base, the ship landed at an eerie scientist lab. It was where we met a computer simulation . . . he said his name was Blake. He was rather good-looking, and also a hologram. Drake was recognized as a threat to him, which instantly put him on our side, I think. He tried to set up a defense system against Drake, but it was to no avail. All he really did was make a lot of noise. It was quite unfortunate . . . he seemed really helpful. And did I mention good-looking? I know I saw a hologram, but like . . . wow. If he was a real guy, I'd like to get to know him more. Maybe then I could practice flirting. . . . uh . . . Moving on, the ship stopped at an old mall after. I only investigated two shops in there with Noriko. It was pretty bland, but having her there livened the mood a whole lot. She's a really nice person. . . . (edited)
She was affected pretty badly from the motive. She said she couldn't see very well, and was pretty freaked out by it all. I wanted to comfort her, but seeing that I was pretty bad at that sort of thing, I was sure she'd remain stagnant. Instead . . . she thanked me. It made me feel all giddy. I haven't felt that way ever since I left my boarding school in sixth grade. I forgot how nice it felt to be a friend to someone, and have them be the same friend to you. I hope we remain this close for the unseeable future ahead of us. When the motive ended, Ema and Luna were found dead. Turns out that Tokumei was responsible. He did it out of respect and fear for Ema, as Luna was hurting her. I admire such nobility. . . .
Then we moved from the mall to an abandoned amusement park. I went to go look around, but not before chatting with David sometime previously. He offered loaning my family some money to get them from where they are in Mexico. I am a little uncomfortable with such generosity . . . especially from a junior with a much more successful life than me . . . but it's not for me. It's for them. With such money, my father could actually get to America and find a stable job. My siblings could move from Mexico to America too along with the rest of my relatives. I know they'd appreciate it. Last but not least, I want to apologise to Jack. He really is trying around here, and has seen better results than me. He isn't a fool at all. But I still am. I'm sorry Jack.
