Asteroid Coaster, anyone? No, that's not name of the rollercoaster - there's no sign telling you what the name of it should be (although it really should). Walking through the waiting line tells you that it was space themed, interestingly enough - you can see the cabin and mountain vibe still appear here and there, though. Going up to the loading station reveals that this place is still running as if it was a normal day. PSAs are being played, trains are being sent out... in fact, you could easily hop on and enjoy the ride. The ride first takes up and around the mountain the waiting line was in, leading to a drop into a hole where the peak of the mountain is. Things get considerably odder here, with the inside of the mountain being more space-y - it's dark, and you can see stars and asteroids everywhere. Going deeper in makes the coaster go into one of the asteroids (or, well, a huge one), revealing a lab of sorts inside of it. Some sort of green chemical is being held inside tubes, although it's impossible to make out what it is since you're going too fast to stop. The rollercoaster then goes back into space, but into a rainbow-themed space - neon ships fill the skies, flashing bright and pretty colors that thankfully don't flash too quickly. Honestly, the more you look at it, the more it looks like a carnival in space - there are some bright neon signs like those in some carnivals, and there's even fireworks going on, it seems. Eventually, the coaster goes through a ship and quickly exits out back into the outdoor mountain area, taking you to the unloading station. It's a somewhat intense and fast ride all around, although it isn't really that intense. No loops are present on the ride. Odd. (edited)
