Its a game shop, or well, what remains of one anyways... It's a unit strewn with toppled racks, loose shelves, destroyed merchandise that's incredibly unrecognizable today and some rubble of its own. There's also a hole in the ceiling, a small, torn-apart concrete pillar occupying that particular space, allowing people to see into the store directly above. It seems that that very same pillar was blocking the path for the next room inside the premises. Enough about the place itself, let's talk about the games. They're numerous in terms of their quantity, these plastic cases holding disks are singlehandedly the most amount of junk available there and would likely be the first thing that people would direct their attention towards in terms of detail. As for quality however, they're hilariously obsolete. The covers for those boxes were often times discolored, if not gone completely. Notable ones that were preserved were inmaculate titles such as "Minekraft", "Battlezone 3", "Dungunronpa" and many more. The disk they hold in their cavities are too scratched and dirty to be able to play. Disks are a thing of the past nowadays but they're still very much applicable in modern day device, thanks to the available ports. Problem was that, not only does the airship not harbor such an equipment, these disks seem to be tailored for a time long since passed. A few other things you might notice would be these rectangular boxes of different shapes and colors. Most of them aren't exactly intact but those that were seemed to resemble 'consoles' back in the day. Their accompanying controllers and other forms of handhelds can be found all over the store as well, contributing to the mess.
